# LinkedIn Post

Software engineers are learning to become electricians.

That's not a joke. I've been in tech for over 10 years, and this is the first time I'm hearing people seriously talk about leaving the industry entirely.

Layoffs everywhere. AI replacing workflows overnight. April H1B lottery coming up again — and the anxiety is thicker than ever.

I get it. I've been there.

Years ago, I didn't get picked in the H1B lottery. Multiple times. The anxiety wasn't about the difficulty — it was about the unknown. You don't know what tomorrow looks like, but you have a feeling it won't be good.

That kind of uncertainty is paralyzing.

Here's what helped me — and it's embarrassingly simple:

𝗦𝘁𝗲𝗽 𝟭: Figure out what you actually want.
Not what LinkedIn tells you to want. Not what your parents want. What YOU want.

𝗦𝘁𝗲𝗽 𝟮: Break it down into small daily tasks. Write them down.
This matters more than you think. There's a concept in psychology called the Zeigarnik Effect — unfinished tasks keep running in the background of your brain like apps you forgot to close. Your phone gets slower. Your brain gets foggier. But the moment you write it down, your brain treats it as "handled." The background apps close.

𝗦𝘁𝗲𝗽 𝟑: Do the tasks. Cross them off.
The feeling of crossing something off is addictive. Each one gives you a tiny bit of control back. Anxiety gets squeezed out, one checkmark at a time.

I learned this method 7 years ago from a Harvard student's vlog. I've done it every single day since. Same notebook format, same routine.

Here's the thing about anxiety: you can't think your way out of it. But when your hands are busy, your brain doesn't have time to spiral.

Anxiety is infinite. Your to-do list is not.

For those going through the H1B lottery this April, or navigating layoffs, or just feeling the weight of uncertainty — you're not alone. And the best thing you can do right now is pick one small thing and do it today.
