"Finding a cofounder is harder than finding a spouse."

I used to think this was hyperbole. After 10+ years building data and AI products, I think it UNDERSTATES the difficulty.

Sam Altman's framework is deceptively simple — your cofounder needs to be:

1. Smart
2. Gets things done
3. Someone you genuinely want to spend time with

Finding two out of three? Easy. All three? Almost impossible.

Here's why this matters MORE in 2026:

Small teams with AI can now outperform teams 10x their size. That means your cofounder choice isn't just important — it's EVERYTHING.

When a 3-person team can ship what used to take 30, the quality of those 3 people becomes the only variable that matters.

From 10 years of building data and AI products, the best teams I've seen were always tiny and obsessively focused. They said NO to 97 out of 100 things. They didn't hire until it hurt.

Airbnb had TWO employees after their first year. Not because they couldn't hire — because they understood that every early hire either multiplies or divides your velocity.
