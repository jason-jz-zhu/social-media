Facebook didn't grow because of virality. Alex Schultz, who ran growth at Facebook for years, says Facebook was actually NOT viral. It grew through pure word of mouth.

That surprised me. But the bigger insight is this: growth doesn't matter AT ALL until you have retention.

I'm revisiting YC's lecture series against a decade of building data and AI products, and Schultz's retention framework is the most underappreciated idea in startups. He says plot your retention curve by cohort. If it flattens out parallel to the X-axis, you have product-market fit. If it trends toward zero, NOTHING you do on growth will save you.

You don't even need scale to see this. Ten thousand users is enough to read the curve shape. I've seen this firsthand building data products — the teams that obsessed over acquisition before retention burned through users like fuel. The ones that got 30 people to stick around forever? Those were the ones worth scaling.

His other counterintuitive move: stop optimizing for power users. Focus on the marginal user — the one who almost churned, almost didn't come back. That's where growth lives.
