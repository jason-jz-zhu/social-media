Google makes more profit than the entire U.S. airline industry combined. Airlines move BILLIONS of people every year. Google runs a search box.

The difference? Airlines are in perfect competition. Google is a monopoly. And Peter Thiel argues there's almost NOTHING in between.

I'm revisiting his YC lecture against a decade of building data and AI products, and the part that hit hardest wasn't the monopoly theory itself — it was how EVERYONE lies about their market.

Non-monopolists shrink their definition: "We're the only British food restaurant in Palo Alto." Sounds unique. Still a restaurant in a brutal market.

Monopolists expand theirs: Google calls itself a tiny player in the $500B advertising market. Meanwhile they own 90% of search.

Thiel's real playbook? Start absurdly small. Amazon started with books. PayPal started with 20,000 eBay power sellers. Facebook started with 10,000 Harvard students. Dominate a tiny market, then expand in concentric circles.

In AI right now, everyone's building "horizontal platforms." The winners will be the ones who pick one narrow wedge and OWN it first.

Competition is for losers. Monopoly is the goal. But the path starts smaller than anyone wants to admit.
