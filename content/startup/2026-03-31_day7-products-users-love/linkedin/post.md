Wufoo had 500,000 users and a support team of 10. Average response time: 7-12 minutes.

No AI chatbot. No outsourced call center. Just 10 people who all did customer support — including the engineers.

Kevin Hale calls this Support Driven Development. Everyone at Wufoo handled tickets. The result: $118K in total funding, acquired by SurveyMonkey for a 29,561% return.

I'm revisiting YC's lecture series against a decade of building data and AI products, and Hale's framework reframed something I thought I understood about growth. He argues that growth = conversion rate minus churn. Most teams pour resources into conversion. But reducing churn by 1% has the SAME effect as increasing conversion by 1% — and it's dramatically cheaper.

His metaphor is striking: acquiring new users is DATING. Retaining them is a MARRIAGE. And most companies put their B-team on the marriage.

The part that hit hardest: Gottman's research on relationships maps directly to products. The number one predictor of early failure is not bugs, not bad design — it's STONEWALLING. Not responding to your users. Silence kills products the same way it kills marriages.
