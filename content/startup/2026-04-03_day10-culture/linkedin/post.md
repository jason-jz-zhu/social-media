Brian Chesky spent 5 MONTHS finding Airbnb's first engineer. Not because nobody applied. Because he believed the first hire implants a DNA chip into the company that never comes out.

I'm revisiting YC's startup lecture series against a decade of building data and AI teams, and Day 10 stopped me cold. Alfred Lin (Sequoia, ex-Zappos COO) and Chesky lay out a framework where culture isn't a poster on the wall — it's a daily fitness routine. You can't crash-diet your way to great culture.

The data backs this up: companies on Fortune's "Best Places to Work" list returned 11.8% annually vs roughly 6% for the S&P 500. Culture isn't soft. It's a compounding financial advantage.

The most counterintuitive move? Airbnb wrote their core values BEFORE hiring anyone. And when the Samwer brothers cloned Airbnb with 400 employees in 30 days, Chesky refused to acquire them. Missionaries over mercenaries. Europe now generates over 50% of Airbnb's revenue.
