Tyler Bosmeny cold-emailed 400 school districts in 2 months to get Clever's first customers.

Not 40. Four hundred. And only 2.5% took the call.

I'm revisiting YC's startup lectures against 10+ years of building data and AI products, and Lecture 19 is a reality check on sales: the founder IS the sales team. Passion and domain knowledge beat "sales experience" every time. Don't wait to hire a salesperson — you ARE the salesperson.

The counterintuitive part: the best salespeople talk 30% of the time and listen 70%. The pitch isn't about your product. It's about their problem. "Why did you take my call?" and "How do you solve this today?" — those two questions close more deals than any demo.

And the 30-second pitch? Three sentences: what you do (so your mom understands), how big the market is, and what traction you have. That's it. Everything else is follow-up.

The framework that stuck with me: follow-up isn't harassment. It's confidence in your product. Bosmeny's team ran 60+ touchpoints for a single deal. "Maybe" is what kills you — get to Yes or No fast.
