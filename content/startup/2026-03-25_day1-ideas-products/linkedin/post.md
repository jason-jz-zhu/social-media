Google was the 13th search engine. Facebook was only for college kids. Airbnb asked you to sleep on a stranger's couch.

Every great startup idea sounded terrible at first.

I'm revisiting YC's 20-lecture startup playbook and pressure-testing it against 10 years of building in tech. Day 1 from Sam Altman: if your idea sounds obviously good, you're already too late.

The sweet spot? An idea that sounds bad to most people — but you have specific domain insight into why it's actually good.

Having built data and AI products, I've seen this firsthand. The ideas that got the most pushback internally were often the ones that created the most value.
