Enterprise IT is a $3.7 TRILLION market. Consumer tech? $170 billion. Yet every startup wants to build the next Instagram.

I'm revisiting YC's lecture series against 10+ years of building data and AI products for enterprises, and Day 12 hit different — because this is MY domain. Aaron Levie (Box) gave this talk in 2014, arguing it was "the best time ever for enterprise software" thanks to cloud and mobile disruption. He was right then.

But here's what's interesting: his core framework still works perfectly in 2026, you just swap the technology layer. In 2014, the gap was between cloud-native possibilities and legacy on-prem reality. In 2026, the gap is between what AI makes possible and how enterprises still operate. Same pattern, new disruption cycle.

His sharpest insight: find the WIDEST GAP between "what technology makes possible" and "how things actually work today." That gap is your startup opportunity. And right now, that gap is wider than it's been since the cloud transition.
