Your company can only move as fast as the number of people who can take an idea from concept to shipped product.

Not engineers. Not headcount. Not budget. BARRELS — Keith Rabois's term for the rare people who can drive a project all the way through without anyone else pushing them.

I'm revisiting YC's startup lecture series against 10+ years of building data and AI products, and Rabois's "How to Operate" talk hit different. His core framework: CEO = editor, not writer. Your job is to simplify, clarify, and allocate — not to do the work yourself. Every step you remove from a process improves performance by 30-50%.

But here's what stuck with me most: simplifying the PROCESS is not the same as simplifying the PROBLEM. In data and AI, I've watched teams dumb down the problem to make execution easier. The best operators do the opposite — they keep the problem complex and make the execution path radically simple.

His delegation 2x2 (conviction x consequence) is something I wish I'd had a decade ago. Would have saved me from micromanaging things that didn't matter and delegating things that did.
