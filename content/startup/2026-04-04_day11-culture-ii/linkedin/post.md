Stripe's first designer was an 18-year-old in Sweden. Their first CTO was still in college. The Collison brothers didn't lower the bar — they redefined where to look.

I'm revisiting YC's startup lecture series against a decade of building data and AI teams, and Day 11 reframed how I think about early hiring. Patrick and John Collison (Stripe) and Ben Silbermann (Pinterest) argue that your first 10 hires aren't just filling roles — they're hiring the next 100 people by proxy. Culture is how decisions get made when you're NOT in the room. And the number of decisions you can be in the room for decreases exponentially as you grow.

The most actionable insight: think like a value investor. Don't compete for "consensus talent" that everyone already knows is great. Find people whose ACTUAL talent far exceeds their APPARENT talent. The best early hires are undervalued assets hiding in plain sight.

One red flag that stuck with me: if a candidate lists 7 unrelated companies they'd love to work for, they want "experience" not your mission.
