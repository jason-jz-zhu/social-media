# Medium Platform Rules

## Formatting

- Full Markdown supported (headings, bold, italic, lists, blockquotes, code blocks)
- H1 is the title (only one per article)
- H2 and H3 for body structure — use generously for scannability
- Subtitle field is separate from the body and carries high SEO weight
- Embed support for tweets, YouTube, Gist, and other platforms

## SEO Weight

- **Title:** Highest weight — include primary keyword near the front
- **Subtitle:** Second highest weight — use secondary keywords, expand the promise
- **First 100 words:** Search engines and Medium's algorithm index this heavily
- **H2 headings:** Moderate weight — include keywords where natural
- **Alt text on images:** Indexed by search engines

## Read Time Sweet Spot

- **7-12 minute read time** is the algorithm sweet spot on Medium
- This corresponds to roughly 2000-3000 words
- Articles shorter than 5 minutes get less distribution
- Articles longer than 15 minutes see higher bounce rates

## Images

- Include 1-3 images per article
- Every image must have descriptive alt text (accessibility + SEO)
- Feature image (first image) appears in previews and social cards
- Avoid stock photos that feel generic — screenshots, diagrams, and original visuals perform better

## Algorithm and Distribution

- **First 48 hours are critical:** claps, responses, and read ratio in the first 48 hours determine whether Medium's algorithm boosts the article
- Share immediately after publishing to drive early engagement
- Respond to every comment in the first 48 hours
- Articles with higher read-through rates (people finishing the article) get more distribution

## Publications

- Submit articles to Medium publications for extra reach
- Publications have their own follower bases and can significantly amplify distribution
- Target publications relevant to content pillars: AI, tech, career, productivity
- Follow each publication's submission guidelines and formatting requirements

## Engagement Best Practices

- End with a question to encourage responses
- Use bold and pull quotes to highlight key insights for skimmers
- Break up long paragraphs — aim for 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- Use numbered lists and bullet points for practical/actionable sections

## Publishing Cadence

- Once per week, Tuesday through Thursday morning ET
- Avoid publishing on weekends — lower traffic and engagement
- Consistent weekly cadence signals to the algorithm that you are an active creator
