# Decomposition Guide

How to decompose mother content (Substack newsletter) into platform-specific pieces.

## Rule 1: Never Translate, Always Rewrite

Content for each platform must be written natively for that platform. Never take the Substack article and translate it into Chinese for XHS, or copy-paste paragraphs into a LinkedIn post. Each platform gets a fresh piece of writing that happens to share the same core insight.

## Rule 2: Each Platform Gets a Unique Angle

From the same core insight, extract a different angle for each platform:

- **Substack:** Full depth — the complete argument with nuance, data, and personal narrative
- **Medium:** SEO-optimized — title targets a search query, content structured for discoverability
- **LinkedIn:** Professional takeaway — 1-2 points distilled into a career/industry insight
- **YouTube:** 口播脚本 — conversational Chinese narration, visual storytelling structure
- **小红书:** 情感故事 — personal story entry point, carousel-friendly structure, saveable frameworks

## Rule 3: Chinese and English Are Independent Content Systems

Chinese content (XHS, YouTube) is more emotional and story-driven. English content (LinkedIn, Medium, Substack) is more data and insight-driven.

- Chinese: lead with feelings, personal scenes, relatable struggles, then deliver the insight
- English: lead with the insight or data point, support with evidence, add personal color

These are not style preferences — they reflect how audiences on each platform consume content.

## Rule 4: Concrete Example

**Topic: "$450/month on AI tools — is it worth it?"**

| Platform | Angle | Opening |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Substack | Full cost-benefit analysis of AI tool spending for knowledge workers | "I spent $450 last month on AI subscriptions. Here's my honest breakdown..." |
| Medium | "How to Evaluate Whether AI Tools Are Worth the Investment" (SEO) | "The average knowledge worker now spends $200-500/month on AI tools..." |
| LinkedIn | ROI framing — what $450 buys in productivity | "My AI tool stack costs $450/month. My hourly rate went up by 3x." |
| YouTube | 中文口播："我每月花3000块订阅AI工具，值吗？" | "大家好，今天想聊一个让我老婆翻白眼的话题..." |
| 小红书 | 个人故事："每月450刀订阅AI，我老婆问我值吗" | "昨晚交完信用卡账单，我老婆看了一眼：'你又花了450刀在AI上？'" |

## Rule 5: Not Every Topic Goes to Every Platform

Some topics are naturally platform-specific:

- **H1B/签证/在美华人生活** → XHS + YouTube only (Chinese audience cares, English audience doesn't)
- **Industry analysis / tech trends** → Substack + Medium + LinkedIn only
- **Personal career story** → All platforms (universal appeal, different angles)
- **Tool tutorials** → Medium + XHS (both audiences search for how-to content)

The `shared/outline.md` platform angles section should explicitly mark which platforms to target for each topic.
