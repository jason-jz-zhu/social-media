# Substack Platform Rules

## Character Limits

- **Email subject line:** 50 characters max (shows in inbox; shorter = higher open rate)
- **Preview text:** 100 characters max (the gray text after the subject in inbox view)
- **Body:** No hard limit, but 2500-4000 words is the sweet spot for this newsletter

## Formatting

- Full Markdown supported (headings, bold, italic, lists, blockquotes, code blocks)
- Images supported inline
- External links allowed everywhere — Substack is a private domain with no link penalties
- Footnotes supported
- Buttons/CTAs can be embedded

## Email Deliverability Best Practices

- Keep subject lines under 50 characters to avoid truncation on mobile
- Avoid spam trigger words in subject lines (FREE, ACT NOW, URGENT)
- Use preview text intentionally — if left blank, Substack pulls the first line of body text
- Consistent send schedule (same day, same time) trains inbox algorithms to deliver
- Encourage replies — email providers rank senders higher when recipients reply
- Avoid excessive images in the first scroll — some email clients block images by default
- Plain text formatting in the opening paragraph improves rendering across email clients

## Engagement Drivers

- **"Reply to this email"** is the highest-value CTA — it signals to email providers that the sender is trusted, improves deliverability, and builds direct reader relationships
- **"Forward to a friend"** is the primary organic growth mechanism on Substack
- Ask one clear question at the end to prompt replies
- Use the opening line to hook — many readers only see the first 2-3 lines in their inbox

## Subscriber Growth

- Every Medium article should end with a CTA linking to the Substack
- LinkedIn posts can reference "my newsletter" with a link in comments
- Substack's own recommendation network helps with discovery
- Free posts get wider distribution than paid-only posts

## Publishing Cadence

- Every Thursday at 9:00 AM ET
- Consistency matters more than perfection — never skip a week without notice
